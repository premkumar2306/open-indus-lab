# Competing Hypotheses — Plain Summary

This platform models six distinct positions on the Indus-Harappan script.
None is pre-selected as correct. All are testable against the same corpus.

---

## VPS2024 — Proto-Tamil (Ponmuthu Shanmugham)
**Claim**: The script encodes ancient Tamil.
**Method**: Maps graphemes to Tamil phonemes; tally marks as phonetic placeholders; motifs as urban street identifiers.
**Key evidence**: 400+ seal readings in Tamil with consistent semantic domains; *Tholkaappiyam* grammar support; graffiti mark continuity (Rajan & Sivamantham 2025).
**Extends**: Mahadevan (1977), Parpola (1994), Jeeva (2020).
**Status**: Active. Submitted for peer review 2026.

---

## MAHADEVAN — Dravidian concordance (Iravatham Mahadevan, RMRL)
**Claim**: Script encodes Proto-Dravidian. Most frequent sign (jar/cow) is first decipherment key.
**Method**: Systematic concordance of all signs across the corpus.
**Key evidence**: Standard sign numbering system still in use. RMRL corpus at www.indusscript.in.
**Status**: Active. Foundation for most subsequent research.

---

## PARPOLA — Proto-Dravidian logo-syllabic (Asko Parpola, Helsinki)
**Claim**: Script is logo-syllabic Proto-Dravidian using the rebus principle.
**Method**: Comparative Dravidian linguistics; extensive corpus work.
**Key evidence**: *Deciphering the Indus Script* (CUP, 1994); CISI Vol.1 & 2.
**Status**: Active. Most widely accepted academic framework.

---

## FSW2004 — Non-linguistic emblems (Farmer, Sproat, Witzel) — NULL HYPOTHESIS
**Claim**: Signs are political/religious emblems, not speech-encoding.
**Evidence cited**: High singleton rate; brevity; no complex syntax; no bilingual text.
**VPS2024 rebuttal**: Brevity = purpose (identifier/permit not essay); singletons explained by modifier system; long-form writing on perishable materials (cotton cloth, wooden planks) not survived; seal 2617 implies banner reading not seal reading.
**Status**: Active. Functions as the null hypothesis for all statistical tests.

---

## RAO2009 — Language-like statistical structure (Rao, Yadav et al.)
**Claim**: Indus signs show conditional entropy consistent with natural languages.
**Method**: Entropy analysis, n-gram statistics.
**Key evidence**: *Science* 324:1165 (2009). DOI: 10.1126/science.1170391.
**Status**: Active. Does not identify a specific language — provides statistical baseline.

---

## JEEVA2020 — Proto-Tamil (Purnachandra Jeeva)
**Claim**: Script encodes ancient Tamil. Tamil syllabic structure identified.
**Method**: 30+ years of sign-by-sign analysis in Tamil.
**Key evidence**: *sinthuveLiyil munthu thamiz* (ISBN 978-81-9427-9105, 2020, Tamil).
**Relationship to VPS2024**: VPS2024 extends and modifies Jeeva's phonemes and morphemes, adding tally mark analysis, modifiers, motif topology, and increased information density.
**Status**: Active.
